UTM Parameters, gclid & fbclid — Explained

This is a long-ish article on purpose — use it to practice GTM's built-in "Scroll Depth" trigger (Triggers → New → Scroll Depth, 25/50/75/90%).

When you run a Facebook or Google ad, the click doesn't just land the user on your page — it can carry data with it, in the URL itself. That data is what lets Google Tag Manager, GA4, and your ad platforms figure out which campaign, which ad, and which keyword actually drove a visit or a sale.

UTM parameters

utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign, utm_term, and utm_content are five tags you manually add to the end of a URL, e.g.:

https://yourstore.com/?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=summer_sale

This site captures all of these automatically (see captureUTMs() in js/script.js) and stores them for the session, then attaches them to the purchase and generate_lead events — so on the Thank You page you can see exactly which campaign "gets credit" for a sale.

gclid and fbclid

gclid is auto-added by Google Ads to every ad click (auto-tagging) — it's what Google Ads uses internally to match a conversion back to the exact ad and keyword. fbclid is Meta's equivalent, added automatically when someone clicks a Facebook/Instagram ad.

You don't need to add these manually — they show up on their own the moment someone clicks a real ad. Try it: take this site's live URL, add ?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=test1 to the end, open it, add something to cart, and check the Thank You page or the Events Log page.

Why this matters for retargeting

Once GTM fires a remarketing tag (Facebook Pixel / Google Ads remarketing) on every page view, anyone who visited — regardless of whether they bought — becomes part of an audience you can advertise to again. Combine that with UTM data and you can tell which channel brought visitors who didn't convert, and retarget them specifically.
